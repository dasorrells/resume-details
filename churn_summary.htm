CODE & PROCESS SUMMARY

1. Data Preparation & Feature Engineering

  • Data Collection & Cleaning: Gathered historical service usage, purchase transactions, and loyalty plan details from several Snowflake tables, and using SQL developed a new dataframe that defines an anchor date and calculates history from this; replaced infinite values with NaN and imputed missing entries using medians (numeric) or modes (categorical), from
  • Feature Creation: Engineered key behavioral signals, including recency of visits, time-windowed spend (3M/6M/12M), and service-delay ratios, to capture leading indicators of churn risk. Worked extensively with our leading experts in operation to capture relevant features.
  • Scaling & Transformation: Applied custom weighting to critical features (e.g., days since last service, membership redemption) and MinMaxScaler to produce normalized inputs for machine learning.

2. Model Training & Scoring

  • Train/Val/Test Split: Stratified splits ensured the natural 2025% churn rate was preserved across training, validation, and test sets.
  • Algorithm Selection & Optimization: Evaluated various ML frameworks including Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, Ensemble Techniques, and Neural Networks; tuned hyperparameters (via Grid/Randomized Search) for maximum recall on the minority (churn) class.
  • Scoring Pipeline: Deployed a fully automated workflow that processes incoming data, applies the same transformations, and outputs churn risk scores on each record. By refining thresholds via ROC and precision-recall analysis, the pipelines latest iterations captures ~80% of customers that will not return in 90 days, despite this target variable being 20-25% on average.

3. Behavioral Segmentation & Targeting

  • Clustering: Used K-Means to group high-risk customers into segments, guided by elbow and silhouette methods to choose the optimal number of clusters. Each segment surfaces unique behavioral patternslike frequent low-ticket visits vs. infrequent high-spend visits.
  • Segment-Specific Campaigns: Marketing teams develop tailored messaging and offers (e.g., discounts for value-driven customers, product bundles for high-spend visitors) to maximize engagement.
  • Control Groups: Within each cluster, 10% of customers are held out as control, ensuring reliable, segment-by-segment measurement of uplift from targeted interventions.

4. Measurement & Business Impact

  • A/B Testing & Statistical Significance: Rigorous comparison of test vs. control performance (e.g., retention, average spend, average frequency) provides stronger POV to the true effects of each promotional strategy.
  • Iterative Feedback Loop: Insights from segment outcomes inform model updates, feature refinements, and new marketing hypotheses, fueling continuous improvement in churn prevention.
  • ROI & Retention Gains: Decreasing churn within identified segments drives increased visit frequency and higher customer lifetime value, with marketing resources optimally directed at the highest-risk, highest-impact customers.

Bottom Line: Through careful data engineering, robust model development, strategic clustering, and controlled experimentation, this end-to-end churn prevention framework provides marketing with timely, accurate lists of at-risk customerssupporting targeted campaigns, measurable outcomes, and significant improvements in customer retention. Bottom line is improved retention, more targeted messaging, and material EBITDA dollars.
