Guest-Level Customer Lifetime Value & Segmentation Framework

“Designed a guest-level SQL and Python framework to model lifetime revenue, visit cadence, and segmentation, integrating behavioral buckets with acquisition cohorts to power strategic CLV forecasting and retention insights.”

1. Objective

To develop a unified analytics pipeline that captures and segments customer behavior across years of visit history, creating a value ladder view that informs strategic retention, loyalty, and growth planning.

2. Data Engineering (SQL)

  • Used CTEs to define guest acquisition dates, transaction cadence, redemption patterns, and long-term engagement over 1, 2, 3, 5, and 10-year windows
  • Rolled transaction activity into structured visit-level summaries by guest, body part, and time period
  • Segmented by first-visit revenue, visit recurrence, time between visits, and Wax Pass activity

3. Feature Generation (Python)

  • Binned guests into behavioral groups based on visit count, gap between visits, service variety, retail add-ons, and loyalty depth
  • Created categorical flags for fast returners (e.g. 60/90/180-day second visits), Wax Pass usage, and spend mix by service type
  • Defined CLV quartiles and applied dimension-by-metric profiling across acquisition cohorts and value buckets

4. Visualization & Output

  • Generated waterfall charts to illustrate guest journey from first visit to long-term retention
  • Exported segmented CLV summaries by cohort and behavioral tag to guide pricing, marketing, and operational strategy
  • Enabled repeatable benchmarking of revenue per guest by behavior, acquisition class, and structural traits

Bottom Line

This guest-level CLV framework combines detailed SQL lifecycle modeling with Python-based feature engineering to enable rich behavioral segmentation and accurate revenue forecasting. It serves as a strategic asset for defining value tiers, retention risk, and growth potential at scale.
