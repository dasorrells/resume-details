Laser Service Adoption Analytics Framework

“Built a robust SQL and Python-based framework to evaluate laser service adoption, segment guest behavior, and quantify trailing purchase patterns and lifetime value across service categories.”

1. Objective

The goal was to understand behavior and value of guests adopting new laser services, track pre-adoption habits, and model trailing engagement across service, retail, and recurring package activity.

2. SQL Data Foundation

  • Used layered CTEs to identify laser package purchasers and unify related events: individual services, redemptions, purchases, and product sales
  • Applied filters to exclude test SKUs, refunds, and consults
  • Joined with profile, location, and event metadata for segmentation and enrichment

3. Python Behavioral Analysis

  • Calculated 365-day trailing spend and visit patterns by guest, segmented by body part and event type
  • Derived total trailing value and repeat behavior, including recurring Wax Pass engagement and retail add-ons
  • Aggregated into summary views by body part to inform product positioning and pricing insights

4. Event Composition Modeling

Built functions to model the composition of guest activity across flexible timeframes (e.g., 7, 30, 90, 365 days), capturing unique combinations of services engaged in the lead-up to laser purchases.

5. Strategic Use Cases

  • Informs targeting logic for future laser campaigns based on historical mix of service behavior
  • Provides inputs for estimating upsell potential from waxers, product buyers, and high-frequency redeemers
  • Establishes baselines for measuring adoption lift and incremental value of laser service rollout

Bottom Line

This analytics system provides a complete view of laser adoption patterns, combining precise SQL data modeling with Python-based segmentation and spend analysis to guide strategic service expansion and upsell strategy.
