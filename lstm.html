Marketing Attribution Modeling Framework

“Engineered a multi-model marketing attribution framework using LSTM and XGBoost to forecast guest acquisition and quantify channel impact, with SHAP-based explainability and temporal feature engineering.”

1. Goal

To develop a comprehensive analytics solution that links multi-channel marketing investments to guest acquisition outcomes, providing a foundation for forecasting, attribution, and strategic budget optimization across various time horizons.

2. Architecture & Inputs

  • Transformed daily campaign-level spend across 12+ media channels into a structured time series panel
  • Engineered supporting features including day-of-week indicators, holiday proximity, trailing spend trends, and prior year volumes
  • Enabled both short- and longer-horizon forecasts to support near-term planning and long-term channel impact evaluation

3. Forecasting & Attribution Models

  • LSTM Neural Network: Designed for sequential modeling across configurable lookback and forecast windows, capturing nonlinear temporal dependencies in acquisition behavior
  • XGBoost Regression: Deployed in parallel to provide fast, accurate predictions and explainable attribution signals using tabular inputs

4. Explainability & Evaluation

To make the models actionable, SHAP was used to quantify feature importance at both global and local levels:

  • SHAP summary plots highlight the most influential spend channels and temporal indicators
  • Local explanations enable diagnosis of performance drivers on specific days or weeks
  • Evaluation metrics include MAE, RMSE, R², and naive confidence intervals to assess prediction accuracy and trustworthiness

5. Strategic Applications

This framework powers testing and optimization of marketing strategy through:

  • Spend shift experiments across platforms (e.g., TikTok vs. Google vs. Meta)
  • Time-based forecasting for campaign pacing and seasonality planning
  • Budget allocation refinement based on expected guest lift or ROI projections

Bottom Line

The attribution system is designed for flexibility and repeatability—supporting a variety of test structures and time windows while preserving model interpretability and actionability. Built entirely in Python, it empowers data-driven marketing with real-world forecasting, channel optimization, and tactical planning tools.
