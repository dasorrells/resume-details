Pricing Analytics Measurement Framework

“Built a reusable statistical framework leveraging clustering, Difference-in-Differences, and Python-based power analysis to measure pricing elasticity and guide future price optimization tests.”

1. Purpose

The framework was developed to evaluate the impact of price changes across a large center network while enabling iterative testing. It was designed to quantify short- and long-term effects on behavior, revenue, and guest retention.

2. Technical Foundation

  • Used statsmodels for statistical power analysis and sample size estimation to determine testing feasibility.
  • Applied k-means clustering to assign matched control groups, reducing variance and increasing statistical precision.
  • Implemented Difference-in-Differences methodology to isolate the causal effect of price changes.
  • Calculated 95% confidence intervals and Cohen’s d to communicate statistical and practical significance.

3. Modular & Reusable Design

The entire pipeline was built using pandas, numpy, and statsmodels, allowing for easy configuration and replication across test waves or new regions. Analysts can plug in future metrics or timeframes without reengineering core logic.

4. Strategic Application

Beyond measuring outcomes of a single test, the framework supports ongoing pricing innovation. It informs which pricing thresholds drive behavior change, guides test design for future iterations, and ensures results are actionable and statistically valid.

Bottom Line

This framework delivers a rigorous, repeatable approach to evaluating price sensitivity, built entirely in Python. By combining clustering, power analysis, and causal inference, it enables confident decision-making and a scalable roadmap for continuous pricing optimization.
